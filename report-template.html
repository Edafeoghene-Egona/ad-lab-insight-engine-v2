Feb 3 to Mar 3, 2026

This report covers numoya's Google Ads from Feb 3 to Mar 3, 2026. The account brought in $420,284 in revenue from $116,126 in ad spend, giving a 3.62x return on every dollar spent, across 8,505 purchases. In the last week (Feb 25 to Mar 4), spending dropped 14% to $29,476. But the ads actually got more efficient: the return on spend went up to 2.94x and the cost per purchase dropped to $16.48. The drop in spending was driven by the algorithm, not a drop in demand. Click-through rate jumped 62% and clicks grew 31%. The top priorities right now are fixing 9 low-quality ads in the Brand Search US campaign, scaling the best-performing keyword "numoya go brush pro" which brought in purchases at just $11.74 each, and correcting a brand name spelling error in the product feed. Two PMAX campaigns (UK/NZ score 26.8, Dryer score 34.0) need urgent attention because their performance collapsed last week.

Key Things to Know

Spending Drop

Spend, Purchases and Revenue All Fell Last Week

Spending last week dropped 14% ($34,291 to $29,476). That pulled purchases down 7.4% (1,932 to 1,789) and revenue down 9.6% ($95,803 to $86,568). The good news: the ads themselves worked better, not worse. Return on spend, cost per purchase, and click rate all improved. We need to check if a budget cap cut off delivery, or if Google's algorithm pulled back on its own.

Fix Needed

9 Ads Are Low Quality in Brand Search US

The Brand Search US campaign has 9 ads that Google has rated as low quality. Low quality ads cost more per click and show up less often. This campaign drove 79 purchases last week so fixing these ads could bring the cost per purchase down without spending more. The ad copy needs a rewrite with more specific product details to get a better quality rating.

Big Opportunity

"numoya go brush pro" is Our Cheapest Source of Sales

The search term "numoya go brush pro" brought in 44 purchases last week at just $14.57 per sale. Over the full month, it drove 203 purchases at $11.74 each. That makes it the most efficient keyword in the whole account. We should protect this by setting a minimum bid and making sure it always has enough budget.
